review on the movies

Octonauts
best series

To this day, it's one of the best shows I've ever watched. I remember watching this when I was younger and I can't stress enough how incredible it is. Even adults can get interested in this dramatic comedy. It's all genuinely educationally, the humour is so hilarious and compelling, it can leave you in a hysterically laughing fit, the characters are extremely endearing and realistic with their flaws and personality, and best of all: It's dramatic. It's suspenseful, compelling, nothing is monotonous--it's all absolutely amazing. Sure, at times the show can step over the line a bit with the tension, I even remember that one of the scenes in an episode made me cry out of fear when I was younger, however, keep in mind I was a complete crybaby. Other shows like Paw Patrol are so unbelievably dumbed-down and educationless, it's a question why the cartoon even made it past the pilot episode, however, Octonaunts combats all of that and forms itself into a down-to-earth comedy. The Creature-Report song/outro gives children an educational yet compelling and subtle recap of everything the Octonaunts have learned throughout their adventure and it's a good thing too since the storyline of every episode is so compelling, you forget it's even a cartoon, unlike shows like MLP whose educational aspect gets in the way of the storyline aspect--the storylines of Octonaunt episodes are absolutely incredible; I'd recommend this any day of the week.

Sonic The Hedgehog
nice for ♾️ time

Videogames adaptations have had a rocky ride. Super Mario Brothers was the nadir for many. Now almost 30 years later, Sonic the Hedgehog whizzes its way onto the screen. The blue furred flyer has fallen from another world and have been living in the remote woods. However Dr Robotnik (Jim Carrey) is on to him. Sonic persuades Tom (James Marsden) the small town sheriff to take him to San Francisco so Sonic can retrieve his gold rings. This is an energetic film. Sonic wisecracks and Carrey really hams it up. Kids would enjoy it the most. It is not up there with Wreck It Ralph but it is a fun but forgettable ride.
